Impact Story

When I joined Varonis, the deployment process was largely manual — engineers provisioning infrastructure by hand, deployments tied to individual knowledge, and no standardized path from code to production. It was slow, error-prone, and impossible to scale.

I led the end-to-end initiative to change that. We migrated from ad-hoc provisioning to a fully codified CI/CD model: Terraform for infrastructure-as-code, Azure DevOps YAML pipelines for every deployment, and a hub-spoke network architecture to give each application stack its own secure, autonomous footprint. We also migrated our core build agents from VMs to AKS — eliminating single points of failure and giving us elastic scale on demand.

The results were tangible. Infrastructure standup time dropped significantly and deployment risk fell because every change followed the same codified, reviewed path. But standardization did something else too — it made the connections between our services and systems visible in a way they hadn't been before.

Documentation had existed in scattered pieces with no cohesion or relationship to each other. Once everything was standardized, it became clear that all of our services and infrastructure were deeply connected — and that those connections deserved to be documented. I initiated the creation of a knowledge base from scratch, giving that structure a home and turning fragmented institutional knowledge into something teams could actually navigate, reference, and build on.
